# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three overlapping audiences arrive at this portfolio:

- **Recruiters and employers** — scanning quickly, need to feel confident that ngoclin.h_ is competent, interesting, and worth reaching out to
- **Peers and fellow developers** — curious, looking for personality and craft; they want to feel like they found someone worth following
- **General public and fans** — discovered the site organically; they're here for the vibe and to get a sense of who this person is

All three should leave with the same impression: this is a real person with a distinct sensibility, not a resume with a domain name.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for ngoclin.h_, a developer and creator who also builds UMIKA (an AI assistant product). The site exists to represent identity, not to sell a product. Success means visitors feel like they met someone — not that they read a brochure.

## Brand Personality

Soft · playful · dreamy

Voice is warm and first-person. The site doesn't announce itself; it invites. Tone leans personal over professional — more handwritten note than pitch deck.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev portfolio**: dark background, green terminal text, skill-bar meters, GitHub contribution tiles, tech-bro minimal
- **Loud startup / SaaS landing**: gradient blob heroes, feature card grids, aggressive CTA buttons, "Join X companies using…"
- **Over-designed agency showreel**: full-page scroll hijack, excessive loading animations, style crushing substance
- **Flat corporate / LinkedIn aesthetic**: professional-gray palette, resume-formatted sections, no personality visible

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the identity** — every design choice should feel like an extension of ngoclin.h_ as a person. If it could belong to any other portfolio, it belongs to none.
2. **Dreaminess earns its place** — whimsy (orbit animations, pastel gradients, playful fonts) is the point, not decoration. Don't sand it down to make it "professional."
3. **No performance mode** — the site is not a pitch. It should feel unforced, personal, and slightly surprising rather than polished into blandness.
4. **Softness with confidence** — playful aesthetics and technical capability coexist. The site doesn't apologize for its tenderness or its competence.
5. **One world across pages** — landing, about, UMIKA, legal — all pages should feel like they live in the same emotional universe, even when their layouts differ.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No strict compliance target. Best-effort: reasonable contrast ratios, keyboard navigability, and semantic HTML. No hard accessibility constraints known.
